    Policy Title:        6.af Policy on Ethical Sourcing of Food and Supplies (PESFS)
    Policy Number:       ST-PO-6AF                                         Date Created:      15-09-2023
    Responsible:         Chief Operational Officer                         Approved by:       Board of Trustees

Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish guidelines for the ethical sourcing of food and supplies for all meetings and
events organized by Canadian University Dubai (CUD). This policy aims to support the University’s commitment to
sustainability, ethical practices, and social responsibility.

Scope
This policy applies to all CUD departments, faculties, staff, and event organizers responsible for planning, procuring,
and managing food and supplies for meetings, conferences, workshops, and other University-hosted events.

Policy Statement
CUD is committed to ethically sourcing food and supplies for meetings and events, prioritizing environmentally
sustainable, socially responsible, and economically viable options. The University supports local businesses, fair trade,
and sustainable agriculture to align purchasing decisions with its values. This approach reduces the environmental
impact by minimizing waste, encouraging the use of sustainable packaging, and promoting reusable or recyclable
products. Key principles guiding this policy include sustainability by prioritizing organic, seasonal, and locally sourced
food; social responsibility by selecting suppliers with ethical labor practices; minimizing environmental impact by
reducing single-use plastics and food waste; and ensuring the availability of healthy, nutritious, and inclusive options
such as vegetarian, vegan, and allergen-free choices to cater to diverse dietary needs.

Procedure
•     Supplier Selection: All suppliers must adhere to CUD’s ethical standards, demonstrating sustainable and ethical
      practices, with preference given to those certified by recognized standards such as Fairtrade, Rainforest Alliance,
      or equivalent.
•     Suppliers should, where practical, confirm compliance with applicable environmental and labor regulations and
      support the University’s sustainability objectives.

•     Food Procurement: CUD will prioritize sourcing food items that are sustainably sourced to support local agriculture,
      minimize environmental impact, and promote ethical procurement practices.
•     Sustainable Packaging: Event organizers must collaborate with suppliers to avoid single-use plastics and prioritize
      biodegradable, compostable, or recyclable packaging to ensure the use of environmentally friendly materials.
•     Preference: should be given, where practical and commercially viable, to reusable, recyclable, or reduced packaging
      options.

•     Minimizing Food Waste: Implement portion control, careful planning, and donation of surplus food to local
      charities, when possible, in compliance with health regulations, while encouraging the use of reusable dishware,
      cutlery, and glassware.
•     Event organizers: should plan quantities carefully and, where feasible, work with suppliers to reduce avoidable
      waste.

•     Diverse Dietary Options: Provide a variety of food options to accommodate different dietary needs, including
      vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, and other special dietary requirements.
•     Training and Awareness: Regular training on ethical sourcing practices will be provided to staff involved in
      procurement and event planning, while suppliers and caterers will be educated about CUD’s commitment to
      sustainable and ethical sourcing.
